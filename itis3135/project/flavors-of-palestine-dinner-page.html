Dinner Recipes:

Mansaf

Ingredients:
  • 2 pounds of lean lamb
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 4 cups of jameed (Middle Eastern yogurt)
  • Parsley
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup of almonds
  • Pine nuts
  • 4-6 loaves of shrak (thin pita bread)
  • 3 cups of short or medium grain rice
  • 2 tablespoons of ghee (clarified butter)
Directions:
  1. Place the lamb in pot on medium heat
  2. Add pepper and chopped onions
  3. Simmer until lamb is brown
  4. Drain the pot, then add water until lamb is completely submerged
  5. Cover pot and cook for 2 1/2 hours
  6. Place the jameed in a sauce pan with a pinch of salt and a teaspoon of olive oil and consistently whisk over medium heat until boiling
  7. Lower the heat of the saucepan and add the cooked lamb and 1/4 cup of lamb broth to the sauce pan
  8. Simmer for 15 minutes and adjust salt to taste
  9. In a skillet add ghee, almonds, and pine nuts and cook until almonds and pine nuts are golden
  10. Cut the bread into 3 inch pieces
  11. Cover your serving tray with the bread
  12. In a boiling pot of water, cook the rice
  13. Lightly place some of the yogurt mix on the bread in the serving tray
  14. Cover the bread with a generous layer of rice
  15. Place the lamb on top of the rice
  16. Add pine nuts and almonds
  17. Add parsley for garnsih
  18. Enjoy!

Maqluba

Ingredients:
  • 1 whole chicken cut into 6 pieces
  • 1 onion cut into 4 pieces
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • Salt
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon powder
  • 2 tablespoon of allspice
  • 1 tablespoon of Middle Eastern seven-spice
  • 1 teaspoonof turmeric
  • 2 eggplants, sliced
  • 1 large cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 3 cups of medium grain rice
Directions:
  1. Deep fry vegetables in vegetable oil
  2. Place the chicken in a pot with the onions
  3. Simmer pot on the stove for a few minutes
  4. Add all of the spices to the pot and add water until the chicken is submerged
  5. Keep pot on medium heat until chicken is completely cooked
  6. Drain the pot, but save the broth
  7. Place the rice in a large pot
  8. Add all of the spices from before to the rice
  9. Put the chicken on top of the rice
  10. Put the eggplant and cauliflower on top of the chicken
  11. Add another layer of rice on top of the chicken
  12. Add broth until it is 1 inch above the rice, add salt on top
  13. Cover the pot and place it on high heat until it is boiling
  14. Lower the heat to low and leave pot for about 20 minutes or until the liquid is evaporated and the rice is cooked
  15. Flip the pot, open side facing down, on to a large serving tray
  16. Using your hands smack on the top and sides of the pot
  17. Slowly lift the pot without turning it
  18. Enjoy!

Msakhan

Ingredients:
  • 4 medium onions, coarsely chopped
  • 4 quarter chicken legs
  • 1 tablespoon of allspice
  • 1 cup of sumac spice
  • 1/2 cup of olive oil
  • 1/4 cup of pine nuts
  • 4 large taboon bread
Directions:
  1. Preheat the oven to 400Â° F
  2. Season the chicken with allspice, salt, and sumac
  3. Add olive oil and chopped onions and place chicken in the oven for 30-45 minutes or until chicken is cooked
  4. Put the chopped onions and olive oil in a pan
  5. Add salt to the pan and cook until the onions are soft and tender, then add sumac at the end
  6. Brush the face of the breads with oil from the onion pan
  7. Place the onions, a sprinkle of sumac, and the pine nuts on the face of the breads as well
  8. Place the chicken over the breads
  9. Enjoy!
