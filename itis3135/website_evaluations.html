Website Evaluations

Stack Overflow

Stack Overflow is a question and answer platform used by millions of people who code. Created by Jeff Atwood and Joeal Spolsky in 2008, Stack Overflow contains questions and answers covering a wide range of different programming languages and topics. The design of the user interface is organized and laid out in a clean and understandable manner for the user.

  • Contrast: From the white background and the black background to the orange and blue boxes in the middle of the page, Stack Overflow follows the principle of contrast. The various font sizes used to get the users attention to flow naturally down the website allows the website to be visually pleasing and engaging.

  • Repetition: Stack Overflow follows the principle of repetition to draw the users attention to different objects on the website. Stack Overflow uses the repetition of different colored blocks with information and a hyperlink to get establish a consistent design pattern.

  • Alignment: Stack Overflow follows the principle of alignment to give the website an organize feel. The side by side of the blocks of colored information helps them pop out and seperate them from the rest of the information on the website. The different rows of statistics about the website help keep the similar information grouped together.

  • Proximity: Stack Overflow follows the principle of proximity to allow the website have a decluttered look and feel for the user. The different rows of similar information helps emphasize and organize the information for the user.

Arngren

Arngren is a Norwegian website that is used to sell an assorment of devices and gadgets. Arngren presents a large collection of items, from electronics to ATVs to bicycles. Despite the simple job of this website, the design of the user interface is disorganize and in shambles.

  • Contrast: Arngren does not follow the principle of contrast causing the page to not have natural flow. Arngren uses a white background for the whole website with random blue, green, and yellow backgrounds thrown in, giving the website an inconsistent look.

  • Repetition: Arngren does not follow the principle of repetition giving the page an inconsistent and confusing look for the user. Arngrens use of differently sized advertisements and differently colored names numbers give the page and uncoordinated and erratic design.

  • Alignment: Arngren does not follow the principle of alignment giving the website a cluttered feel. The randomly placed advertisements and the inconsistent shape and size of the black borders around the advertisements gives the advertisements an out of place and unorganized feel.

  • Proximity: Arngren does not follow the principle of proximity preventing the website from having a decluttered look and feel for the user. Similar advertisements are randomly placed and spread out from each other causing the website to be disorganized, making the user have to search for similar advertisements.
